News Literacy

What is news literacy

News literacy is the acquisition of 21st-century, critical-thinking skills for analyzing and judging the reliability of news and information, differentiating among facts, opinions and assertions in the media we consume, create and distribute.
It is a necessary component for literacy in todays society.



Six Principles of News Literacy

  1. Free expression is the foundation.
  2. Discerning fact from opinion is a basic skill.
  3. When the process of gathering and reporting is transparent, news and information are more meaningful, trusted and credible.
  4. Effective communication of news and information requires synthesis of multiple sources into meaningful context and comprehension of its impact.
  5. Information requires verification to be effective.
  6. Information in todays society must empower forums to give voice to citizens and to monitor the free flow of information.

What Each Prinicple Means

1. The first amendments protect the rights of the press and freedom of speech which allows the press to spread whatever they want. It is up the public that will determine if the news is correct.
2. The public must learn the difference between fact and opinion and when they are being used in a article of news.
3. The reader must find out the motivations of the article to know its purpose.
4. The readers must understand what the informations means and how it is being used to convey information.
5. Readers have to know if the information that they are given is correct or a alternate story compared to the real story.
6. Readers must find reputable source to obtain information so that they feel is honest and trustworthy.
